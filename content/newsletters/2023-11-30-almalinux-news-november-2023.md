Hello everyone! Welcome to this month's AlmaLinux newsletter, where we share the latest AlmaLinux happenings, upcoming events, and more!

## Quick Notes

- AlmaLinux OS 9.3 and AlmaLinux OS 8.9 are now available
- CIS Benchmarks for AlmaLinux 8 have been updated, with an update for AlmaLinux 9 coming soon
- AlmaLinux will return to FOSDEM in 2024, helping host the Distributions Dev Room
- A new guide has been added for moving from CentOS 7 to AlmaLinux 9 using ELevate
- The 2023 election begins December 1st; members will receive email notification
- Recent improvements to the mirror system have been implemented

## Latest from the AlmaLinux Blog

**Announcing AlmaLinux Day Tokyo**

AlmaLinux Day Tokyo will take place on December 9th, following Open Source Summit Japan. Attendees can register for the event or view recordings afterward on the AlmaLinux YouTube channel.

**CIS Benchmarks Update**

The second update to the AlmaLinux OS 8 CIS benchmark (v3.0.0) has been released and tested on AlmaLinux 8.8. Work is beginning on the first update to the AlmaLinux OS 9 benchmark for version 9.2.

**FOSDEM 2024 Distributions Dev Room**

AlmaLinux will maintain a presence at FOSDEM 2024 with booth space and will co-host the Distributions Dev Room.

**AlmaLinux 9.3 and 8.9 Releases**

Both releases represent the first minor versions since transitioning to CentOS Stream in July. Both releases were ready within a week of RHEL's release.

## AlmaLinux Live

Upcoming events include Open Source Summit Japan (December 3-4) and AlmaLinux Day Tokyo (December 9).
